What gets reviewed

The audit is not a vague marketing call. It is a practical review of the parts that usually decide whether a local business gets traffic and inquiries.

Website clarity

Homepage message, offer structure, CTAs, and mobile usability — is the offer obvious in 5 seconds?

SEO & indexing

Technical SEO, schema, sitemap, Search Console signals, service pages, and crawl issues.

GBP alignment

Categories, services, coverage, and whether the website supports what the Google Business Profile is trying to rank for.

Lead & follow-up

Forms, call paths, CRM gaps, and missed follow-up opportunities after inquiries arrive.

Growth opportunities

Referral flow, automation bottlenecks, tracking gaps, and practical next-step recommendations.

Who this is for

This audit is strongest for businesses that live or die on local search, referrals, and direct inquiries.

  • Contractors, handymen, painters, electricians, plumbers, screen repair, and other field-service businesses
  • Businesses with an existing site that is not getting calls, leads, or map-pack traction
  • New businesses that want to launch with the right local structure instead of rebuilding later
  • Owners who want a clear fix-first plan before spending on a rebuild or agency retainer

What you leave with

  1. The biggest blockers first — usually the 3 to 5 issues that most directly affect visibility or conversions.
  2. A realistic next-step recommendation — fix the current site, add key pages, clean up GBP, or rebuild if the structure is too weak.
  3. Package fit if needed — whether the right move is a visibility pass, local launch, growth system, or focused cleanup.
  4. A better decision path — even if the answer is "do not rebuild yet," that is still useful if it saves time and money.
